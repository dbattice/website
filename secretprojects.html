Why Canada should annex the United States

More people make you stronger geopolitically. America is a geopolitical heavyweight due to its large population and high incomes. Growing our population would give us more clout. Integrating more countries together would further science and development. Additionally this is an excuse to talk about devolution and parliamentarism. Canada has parliamentarism and decentralization of immigration welfare education health. Provincial government is better than state government because of parliamentarism and unicameralism, plus independent redistricting commissions. Taxation is about fifty fifty, minus social security. (See Switzerland for how to do social security in a highly decentralized country.) Courts are not as important. Canada has lots of hydro power. The US can build fracking in Canada mitigating problems with Russian gas, climate change, and related issues. Canada gives media regulation to provinces (radio especially). Maximum Canada by Doug Saunders is a similar idea and Noah Smith thinks it is happening. He also points out that Canada should probably do this so they can resist crazy America if it comes to that. Misha Saul also proposed to let Hong Kongers make their own city in Australia.

Coronavirus and lessons from other countries

Conservatives citing Sweden for optimal corona policy in 2020 drove me crazy. Not only because, IF ONLY, but also there is lots to like about Sweden like tax policy, free college, decentralization of the welfare state, pensions, strong local communities, more immigrants than US. Switzerland also has a lot to learn from sicne Switzerland is also very decentralized and federal, but their welfare sate is much better than US. More generally we could learn from Costa Rica about how to not have an army and invest in health and education and they promote ecotourism in a lot of ways. Spain also can teach us stuff about decentralization, parliamentarism is good, younger generations tend to rebel and so you can learn to how to adapt to that, monetary policy is very important. Australia shows how you can have a dominant two party system, with state federal cooperation, and parliamentarism. Their voting system with AV and their senate work much better than America but not ideal, but to be clear, I would take their system in a heartbeat. Mandatory voting is good, and their health system is great, probably the best philosophically and practically for American to copy. Why are Americans so unwilling to ponder what other countries do differently? This reddit thread is amazing and show Americans want walkable places, VATs, minimum mandatory vacation, no tipping, no drug advertising on TV, better more modern banking tech, plausibly quick boiling electric kettles, drinking alcohol in public places as long as you are responsible, prefilled tax returns, bidets.

Long term debt problems in the United States (and welfare state spending)

Mostly an issue of health costs and aging population. on the health front, adopt Finkelstein and Einav plan. Fund with payroll tax initially to prevent too much fiscal disruption, but transition to VAT over time. End so many deductions. See other funding problems. For aging, just need to tweak pension funding a bit. Also doing swiss style social security taxes would be great (equal contributions, no cap). Can even use that as a basis for better child benefits, disability, support for parents, etc. No one knows when the debt will become a problem. Debt was too low in the aftermath of the 2008 crisis. Now it is a problem, and high interest rates are also contributing to the problem. We could look into Swedish or Swiss debt rules if anyone is serious about debt problems. But I think that will require a better welfare state (and those two countries do allow countercyclical spending, aiming to balance the budget over the business cycle). Sweden though arguably has had too little debt in the last twenty years or so, based on interest rates on government bonds and excess unemployment for a while.
